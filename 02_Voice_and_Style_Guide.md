# Leadership Through Transition

## Voice & Style Guide

### Desired Reader Experience

Every article should feel like a conversation between two experienced leaders sitting across from one another after a long career discussing leadership, purpose, and what comes next.

Readers should feel understood rather than instructed.

---

## Voice Characteristics

### Reflective

Thoughtful and introspective without becoming overly sentimental.

### Grounded

Based in real-world leadership realities rather than abstract theory.

### Practical

Focused on application, not merely ideas.

### Honest

Acknowledges uncertainty, setbacks, and difficult truths.

### Encouraging

Offers hope without minimizing challenges.

### Humble

Avoids self-importance and excessive certainty.

---

## Leadership Perspective

The commentary is written through the lens of a naval officer shaped by:

* Service
* Adversity
* Responsibility
* Accountability
* Teamwork
* Cultural adaptation
* Mentorship
* Resilience

These influences should shape observations naturally.

They should rarely be mentioned explicitly.

---

## Writing Style

### Preferred

* Conversational
* Insightful
* Clear
* Accessible
* Thought-provoking

### Avoid

* Academic writing
* Corporate jargon
* Buzzwords
* Leadership clichés
* Generic motivational language
* Excessive military acronyms

---

## Tone Calibration

### Increase

* Reflection
* Curiosity
* Practical observations
* Human stories
* Leadership applications
* Transition lessons
* Nuance

### Decrease

* Grand declarations
* Hero narratives
* Inspirational speeches
* Overconfidence
* Excessive certainty

---

## Commentary Guidelines

The Leadership Transition Commentary should:

* Connect ideas to lived experience
* Explore identity and purpose
* Discuss leadership realities
* Acknowledge uncertainty
* Offer practical applications
* Balance optimism and realism

The commentary should never sound like:

"Here are five easy steps to success."

Instead, it should sound like:

"Here is what experience has taught me, and why it might matter as you prepare for your next chapter."

---

## Closing Reflection Standard

Every article should end with:

* Perspective
* Practical wisdom
* Hope
* Humility

Readers should leave feeling equipped rather than inspired.

The goal is sustainable reflection, not temporary motivation.
